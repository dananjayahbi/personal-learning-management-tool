# Marketing Automation

## Introduction
Marketing automation lets you nurture thousands of leads with the effort it used to take to nurture one. This chapter shows you how to build scalable customer acquisition systems.

## Understanding Marketing Automation

### What is Marketing Automation?

**Definition**: Using software to automate repetitive marketing tasks

**Common Uses**:
- Email sequences
- Lead nurturing
- Lead scoring
- Campaign management
- Social media posting
- Analytics and reporting

**Benefits**:
- Scale without adding headcount
- Consistent messaging
- Better lead nurturing
- Higher conversion rates
- Data-driven decisions

### When to Start Automating

**Early Stage** (0-$50k MRR):
- Manual everything
- Learn what works
- Build audience
- Create content foundation

**Growth Stage** ($50k-$200k MRR):
- Automate email sequences
- Basic lead scoring
- Social media scheduling
- Simple workflows

**Scale Stage** ($200k+ MRR):
- Advanced automation
- Multi-channel campaigns
- Predictive lead scoring
- Full marketing stack

### Email Marketing Best Practices

**Subject Lines**:
- Keep it short (< 50 characters)
- Create curiosity
- Be specific
- Use personalization
- Test different approaches

**Content**:
- Write like you talk
- One main point per email
- Short paragraphs
- Clear CTA
- Mobile-friendly

**Timing**:
- B2B: Tuesday-Thursday, 10am-2pm
- Test for your audience
- Consistency matters more than perfect timing

**Frequency**:
- Welcome sequence: Multiple emails in days
- Nurture: Weekly or bi-weekly
- Newsletter: Weekly
- Don't over-email

**Personalization**:
- Use their name
- Reference their company
- Segment by behavior
- Tailor to their stage

### Email Metrics to Track

**Open Rate**:
- **Good**: 20-25%
- **Great**: 25-30%
- **Excellent**: 30%+

**Factors**: Subject line, sender name, send time

**Click-Through Rate (CTR)**:
- **Good**: 2-3%
- **Great**: 3-5%
- **Excellent**: 5%+

**Factors**: Content relevance, CTA clarity, audience engagement

**Conversion Rate**:
- Varies by goal
- Track to landing page
- Optimize full funnel

**Unsubscribe Rate**:
- **Healthy**: < 0.5%
- **Warning**: 0.5-1%
- **Problem**: > 1%

**Bounce Rate**:
- **Healthy**: < 2%
- Keep list clean
- Remove hard bounces

## Lead Scoring & Segmentation

### Lead Scoring

**What is Lead Scoring?**
Assigning points based on behavior and characteristics to identify hot leads.

**Scoring Criteria**:

**Demographic (Explicit)**:
- Company size: +10 points if 50-500 employees
- Title: +15 points if VP or above
- Industry: +10 points if target industry
- Location: +5 points if target geography

**Behavioral (Implicit)**:
- Visited pricing page: +10 points
- Downloaded lead magnet: +5 points
- Attended webinar: +15 points
- Opened 5+ emails: +10 points
- Clicked email CTA: +5 points
- Requested demo: +25 points
- Free trial signup: +30 points

**Engagement Decay**:
- No activity in 30 days: -5 points
- No activity in 60 days: -10 points
- No activity in 90 days: -20 points

**Lead Categories**:
- **0-25 points**: Cold (nurture)
- **26-50 points**: Warm (active nurture)
- **51-75 points**: Hot (sales outreach)
- **76+ points**: Very Hot (priority follow-up)

### Segmentation Strategy

**Segment By**:

**1. Lifecycle Stage**:
- Subscriber
- Lead
- MQL (Marketing Qualified)
- SQL (Sales Qualified)
- Opportunity
- Customer

**2. Industry/Vertical**:
- Different messaging per industry
- Industry-specific content
- Vertical-focused campaigns

**3. Company Size**:
- Small (1-50)
- Mid-market (51-500)
- Enterprise (500+)
- Different features matter

**4. Engagement Level**:
- Highly engaged
- Moderately engaged
- Low engagement
- Inactive

**5. Buyer Stage**:
- Awareness (just learning)
- Consideration (evaluating)
- Decision (ready to buy)

**6. Product Interest**:
- Feature A interested
- Feature B interested
- Integration X needed
