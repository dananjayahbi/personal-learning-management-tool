## Key Principles

### 1. Be Realistic, Not Optimistic
**Wrong**: "We'll capture 10% of the $50B POS market in Year 1"
**Right**: "We'll serve 50 customers in Year 1, growing to 500 by Year 3"

### 2. Show, Don't Just Tell
**Wrong**: "Our POS system is better than competitors"
**Right**: "Our POS system reduces transaction time by 40% compared to Square, as validated by our current 15 customers"

### 3. Know Your Numbers Cold
You should be able to answer:
- How much revenue in Month 1? Month 6? Year 1?
- What's your customer acquisition cost?
- When will you be profitable?
- How much capital do you need?

### 4. Plan for Multiple Scenarios
- Best case (optimistic)
- Base case (realistic)
- Worst case (pessimistic)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå **Mistake 1: Too Much Fluff, Not Enough Substance**
Don't: Pages of generic business jargon
Do: Specific, actionable strategies with data

‚ùå **Mistake 2: Unrealistic Financial Projections**
Don't: "Year 1: $1M, Year 2: $10M, Year 3: $100M"
Do: Conservative growth based on actual unit economics

‚ùå **Mistake 3: Ignoring Competition**
Don't: "We have no real competitors"
Do: "Square is our main competitor, here's how we differentiate..."

‚ùå **Mistake 4: Vague Target Market**
Don't: "Small businesses who need POS systems"
Do: "Coffee shops and quick-service restaurants with 1-3 locations, $500K-$2M revenue, currently using outdated POS systems"

‚ùå **Mistake 5: No Clear Path to Profitability**
Don't: Hope revenue magically exceeds expenses
Do: Specific timeline: "Break-even at 75 customers in Month 18"

‚ùå **Mistake 6: Writing It and Forgetting It**
Don't: Create plan, put in drawer, never look again
Do: Review monthly, update quarterly, use as living document
