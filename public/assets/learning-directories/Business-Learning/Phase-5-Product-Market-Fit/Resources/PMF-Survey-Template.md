# Product-Market Fit Survey Template

## Overview

This survey uses the Sean Ellis PMF test to measure if you've achieved product-market fit. Send this to customers who have been actively using your product for at least 2-4 weeks.

**Target:** 40%+ responding "Very disappointed" = Strong PMF

## Analysis Guide

### Step 1: Calculate PMF Score

```
PMF Score = (# "Very disappointed" responses / Total responses) √ó 100

Example:
50 responses total
22 said "Very disappointed"
PMF Score = (22 / 50) √ó 100 = 44%

Result: Strong PMF (>40%)
```

### Step 2: Segment by Response

**"Very Disappointed" Group (Your Champions):**
- Who are they? (Look at customer profiles)
- What do they value most? (Q3 responses)
- Who do they recommend you to? (Q2 responses)
- What features do they use? (Q7 responses)

**Action:** This is your Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

**"Somewhat Disappointed" Group (Warm but not hot):**
- Why aren't they "very disappointed"?
- What improvements would move them? (Q4 responses)
- Are they using all features? (Q7 responses)

**Action:** Improve product to convert them to champions

**"Not Disappointed" Group (Wrong fit):**
- Why did they sign up?
- What's missing for them? (Q4, Q5 responses)
- Are they your target market?

**Action:** Either improve for this segment or focus elsewhere

### Step 3: Extract Key Insights

**Value Proposition (from Q3):**

Aggregate responses to find common themes:
- "Saves me 10 hours per week"
- "Caught inventory errors that saved $2,000"
- "Easy to use unlike competitors"

Extract: Your core value in customers' words

**Ideal Customer (from Q2):**

Look for patterns:
- "Small retail stores"
- "Busy restaurant owners"
- "Stores with 5-20 employees"

Extract: Specific target customer description

**Product Improvements (from Q4):**

Count frequency of requests:
- 15 people want "better reporting"
- 12 people want "mobile app"
- 8 people want "QuickBooks integration"

Extract: Prioritized feature roadmap

**Competitive Alternatives (from Q5):**

Understand your competitive set:
- "Would go back to Square" (15 responses)
- "Would use Excel" (8 responses)
- "Nothing else like this" (5 responses)

Extract: Your competitive positioning

## Survey Delivery Best Practices

### Timing

**When to send:**
- After 2-4 weeks of active product usage
- After customer has experienced "aha moment"
- NOT too early (they haven't seen value yet)
- NOT too late (memory fades, bias sets in)

**Frequency:**
- Quarterly for tracking PMF progress
- After major product updates
- When considering pivots

### Delivery Method

**Option 1: In-app survey (Best response rate)**
```
Pop-up or banner in product:
"Quick question: We'd love your feedback!"
[Take 2-minute survey]

Response rate: 30-50%
```

**Option 2: Email survey**
```
Subject: "Quick question about [Product]"

Body:
"Hi [Name],

You've been using [Product] for [timeframe]. We'd love to know 
how you feel about it.

Can you answer one quick question? It takes 30 seconds.

[Link to survey]

Thanks!
[Your name]"

Response rate: 15-25%
```

**Option 3: Personal outreach (Highest quality)**
```
Personal email or call:
"I'm personally reaching out to customers to understand their 
experience. Would you be willing to answer a few questions?"

Response rate: 50-70% (but doesn't scale)
```

### Incentives

**Consider offering:**
- Entry into drawing for gift card ($100-500)
- Discount on next month (10-20% off)
- Extended trial period
- Early access to new features
- Public recognition / case study opportunity

**Don't over-incentivize** (attracts non-serious responses)

## Iteration Plan Based on Results

### If PMF Score < 25% (Weak PMF)

**You don't have PMF yet. Major changes needed.**

**Actions:**
1. Deep dive into "why not disappointed" responses
2. Interview 20+ users about their experience
3. Identify if it's:
   - Wrong customer segment
   - Wrong problem to solve
   - Wrong solution to the problem
   - Poor product execution
4. Consider pivot vs persevere
5. Focus 100% on learning, not scaling

### If PMF Score 25-40% (Emerging PMF)

**You're close but not there yet.**

**Actions:**
1. Analyze "very disappointed" customers deeply
2. Who are they? Double down on this segment
3. What do they love? Amplify those features
4. Why aren't others "very disappointed"? Fix those gaps
5. Iterate product based on feedback
6. Re-survey in 8-12 weeks

### If PMF Score 40-60% (Strong PMF)

**You have PMF! Time to scale.**

**Actions:**
1. Document your Ideal Customer Profile
2. Focus marketing/sales on ICP only
3. Build features for "very disappointed" group
4. Start scaling customer acquisition
5. Monitor PMF score quarterly (maintain 40%+)
6. Expand to adjacent markets once core is solid

### If PMF Score > 60% (Exceptional PMF)

**You have incredibly strong PMF.**

**Actions:**
1. Scale aggressively
2. Raise capital if needed (easy with these numbers)
3. Maintain product quality while scaling
4. Expand team carefully
5. Defend against competitors
6. Consider expanding product line

## Tips for Success

### Do's ‚úÖ
- Send to engaged, active users only
- Keep it short (5 questions max for core survey)
- Ask open-ended follow-ups
- Respond to every respondent ("Thanks for your feedback!")
- Analyze qualitative responses deeply
- Act on feedback and tell customers

### Don'ts ‚ùå
- Don't survey too early (they haven't seen value)
- Don't survey too frequently (survey fatigue)
- Don't ignore negative feedback
- Don't cherry-pick responses
- Don't survey only happy customers (bias)
- Don't forget to close the loop

## Conclusion

The PMF survey is one of the most powerful tools for validating product-market fit. Use it regularly, take the results seriously, and let customer feedback guide your decisions.

**Remember:** 40%+ "very disappointed" is the goal, but the qualitative insights are equally valuable for understanding why and how to improve.
