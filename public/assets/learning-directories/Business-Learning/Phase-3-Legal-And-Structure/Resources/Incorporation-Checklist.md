## Step 1: File Articles of Incorporation

### Delaware C-Corp (Most Common for Startups)

**Method 1: Online Filing (Fastest)**

1. [ ] Go to: [Delaware Division of Corporations](https://corp.delaware.gov/)
2. [ ] Create account
3. [ ] Complete online Certificate of Incorporation
4. [ ] Pay filing fee ($89 + $50 same-day processing optional)
5. [ ] Receive filed certificate (3-7 days standard, same-day if expedited)

**Method 2: Mail/Fax Filing**

1. [ ] Download Certificate of Incorporation form
2. [ ] Complete form (see template below)
3. [ ] Mail or fax to Delaware Division of Corporations
4. [ ] Include check for $89
5. [ ] Wait 7-10 days

**Method 3: Registered Agent Service (Recommended)**

1. [ ] Use service like: Northwest ($29/year), Incfile ($0 + state fee), LegalZoom ($149 + state fee)
2. [ ] They file for you and serve as registered agent
3. [ ] Faster and handles compliance
4. [ ] Recommended: **Northwest Registered Agent** (no upsells)

**Certificate of Incorporation Template** (Delaware):

```
CERTIFICATE OF INCORPORATION
OF
[YOUR COMPANY NAME], INC.

FIRST: The name of the corporation is [Your Company Name], Inc.

SECOND: The address of the corporation's registered office in the State of Delaware is [Registered Agent Address]. The name of its registered agent at such address is [Registered Agent Name].

THIRD: The purpose of the corporation is to engage in any lawful act or activity for which corporations may be organized under the General Corporation Law of Delaware.

FOURTH: The corporation is authorized to issue two classes of stock: Common Stock and Preferred Stock.

The total number of shares of Common Stock the corporation is authorized to issue is 10,000,000 shares, with a par value of $0.0001 per share.

The total number of shares of Preferred Stock the corporation is authorized to issue is 5,000,000 shares, with a par value of $0.0001 per share.

FIFTH: The name and mailing address of the incorporator is:
[Your Name]
[Your Address]

SIXTH: Liability Limitation. To the fullest extent permitted by the Delaware General Corporation Law as the same exists or may hereafter be amended, a director of the corporation shall not be liable to the corporation or its stockholders for monetary damages for breach of fiduciary duty as a director.

SEVENTH: Indemnification. The corporation shall indemnify to the fullest extent permitted by law any person made or threatened to be made a party to an action or proceeding by reason of the fact that such person is or was a director or officer of the corporation.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the undersigned, being the incorporator, has executed this Certificate of Incorporation on [Date].

_______________________
[Your Signature]
[Your Printed Name], Incorporator
```

**Key Decisions**:
- **Authorized shares**: 10M common + 5M preferred (standard, leaves room for growth)
- **Par value**: $0.0001 (minimal, standard)
- **Incorporator**: Can be you; becomes irrelevant after incorporation

### Home State Filing (If Not Delaware)

1. [ ] Go to your state's Secretary of State website
2. [ ] Find "Business Filing" or "Corporations" section
3. [ ] Download Articles of Incorporation form (or file online)
4. [ ] Complete form (similar info to Delaware)
5. [ ] File online or mail with payment ($50-300 depending on state)
6. [ ] Receive approval (1-4 weeks)

**Pros of Home State**:
- Cheaper ($50-150 vs $139+ Delaware)
- No annual Delaware franchise tax
- No registered agent needed (usually)

**Cons of Home State**:
- Less established corporate law
- VCs may require Delaware later (requires conversion, costs $2K-5K)
- Not industry standard for tech startups

## Step 4: Organizational Documents

### A. Bylaws

**What**: Internal rules governing the corporation

**DIY**: Use template below or online (Clerky, Stripe Atlas)

**Lawyer**: Includes in incorporation package ($500-1K)

**Checklist**:
- [ ] Download bylaws template
- [ ] Customize for your company (name, stock, etc.)
- [ ] Review with co-founders
- [ ] Store securely (don't need to file with state)

**Key Bylaws Provisions**:
- Corporate purpose
- Board of directors (size, meetings, quorum)
- Officers (roles and responsibilities)
- Stock certificates and transfers
- Fiscal year
- Amendment procedures

**Standard Defaults**:
- Board meetings: At least annually
- Quorum: Majority of directors
- Stock: Shares issued as board authorizes
- Fiscal year: Calendar year (Jan 1 - Dec 31)

### B. Initial Board Resolution

**What**: First official act of the board

**Actions**:
- [ ] Adopt bylaws
- [ ] Approve stock issuance to founders
- [ ] Appoint officers (President, Secretary, Treasurer)
- [ ] Approve opening bank account
- [ ] Adopt fiscal year
- [ ] Authorize expenses

**Template**:

```
UNANIMOUS WRITTEN CONSENT OF THE BOARD OF DIRECTORS
OF
[YOUR COMPANY NAME], INC.

The undersigned, being all of the directors of [Company Name], Inc., a Delaware corporation (the "Company"), do hereby consent to the following resolutions:

1. ADOPTION OF BYLAWS
RESOLVED, that the Bylaws presented to the Board are hereby adopted as the Bylaws of the Company.

2. ELECTION OF OFFICERS
RESOLVED, that the following persons are elected as officers of the Company:
- President and CEO: [Name]
- Secretary: [Name]
- Treasurer: [Name]

3. AUTHORIZATION TO ISSUE STOCK
RESOLVED, that the Company is authorized to issue [number] shares of Common Stock to the founders as follows:
- [Founder 1 Name]: [number] shares for $[amount]
- [Founder 2 Name]: [number] shares for $[amount]

4. ADOPTION OF STOCK OPTION PLAN
RESOLVED, that the Company adopts the [Year] Stock Option Plan, reserving [number, typically 10-15% of outstanding] shares for issuance.

5. BANK ACCOUNT
RESOLVED, that the Company is authorized to open a bank account at [Bank Name], and [Officer Names] are authorized to execute all necessary documents.

6. FISCAL YEAR
RESOLVED, that the fiscal year of the Company shall be the calendar year ending December 31.

Dated: [Date]

_______________________     _______________________
[Director 1 Signature]      [Director 2 Signature]
[Printed Name]              [Printed Name]
```

### D. 83(b) Election (CRITICAL!)

**What**: IRS form to be taxed on stock value now (low) instead of vesting (higher)

**Why**: Saves huge taxes if company grows

**Deadline**: **30 DAYS from stock grant** (no exceptions!)

**Steps**:
1. [ ] Complete 83(b) election form (template below)
2. [ ] Sign and date
3. [ ] Mail to IRS (certified mail, return receipt) within 30 days
4. [ ] Mail copy to state tax authority
5. [ ] Include copy with tax return
6. [ ] Keep all receipts/proof forever

**Template**:

```
83(b) ELECTION

The undersigned taxpayer hereby elects, pursuant to Section 83(b) of the Internal Revenue Code, to include in gross income the excess (if any) of the fair market value of the property described below over the amount paid for such property.

1. Taxpayer Information:
Name: [Your Name]
SSN: [Your SSN]
Address: [Your Address]

2. Property Description:
[Number] shares of Common Stock of [Company Name], Inc.

3. Date Property Transferred: [Grant Date]

4. Tax Year: [Year]

5. Nature of Restriction: The shares are subject to vesting over [4] years with a 1-year cliff.

6. Fair Market Value: $[FMV per share, typically $0.0001] per share = $[total FMV]

7. Amount Paid: $[amount paid] per share = $[total paid]

8. Income Recognized: $[FMV - Amount Paid] = $0 (if same)

Dated: [Date]

_______________________
[Your Signature]
[Your Printed Name]
```

**Mail To**:
```
Department of the Treasury
Internal Revenue Service Center
[Your local IRS center - check IRS website]
```

**REMINDER SET**: File 83(b) within 30 days (set multiple reminders!)

## Step 6: Founder Equity Documentation

**Required Documents**:

### A. Founders Agreement
- [ ] Equity splits documented
- [ ] Vesting terms agreed (typically 4-year vest, 1-year cliff)
- [ ] Roles and responsibilities defined
- [ ] IP assignment included
- [ ] Departure scenarios covered
- [ ] All founders sign

(See separate Founders Agreement Template)

### B. Stock Purchase Agreements
- [ ] One for each founder
- [ ] Specifies number of shares, price, vesting
- [ ] References vesting schedule
- [ ] Includes company right to repurchase unvested shares
- [ ] Signed by founder and company

### C. Restricted Stock Purchase Agreement
- [ ] If subject to vesting
- [ ] Company can buy back unvested shares at cost if founder leaves
- [ ] Critical to protect company

### D. IP Assignment Agreements
- [ ] All founders assign all IP to company
- [ ] Past, present, and future IP
- [ ] Includes inventions, code, designs, etc.
- [ ] Must be done before or at incorporation

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Missing 83(b) deadline (no exceptions, huge tax bill later)
❌ Not documenting founder equity properly (disputes later)
❌ Forgetting to assign IP to company (ownership issues)
❌ No founders agreement (conflicts inevitable)
❌ Not maintaining corporate records (piercing corporate veil risk)
❌ Missing annual franchise tax (penalties, potential administrative dissolution)
❌ Commingling personal and business funds (IRS issues)
❌ Not getting business insurance (personal liability risk)
