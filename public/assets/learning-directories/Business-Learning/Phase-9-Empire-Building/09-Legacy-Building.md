### 2. The Legacy Timeline

**Short-Term Impact (1-5 years):**
- Your current work
- Immediate results
- People you help today
- Quickly forgotten

**Medium-Term Impact (5-20 years):**
- Your company's trajectory
- Direct mentees and employees
- Industry contributions
- Somewhat remembered

**Long-Term Impact (20-50 years):**
- Institutions you built
- Industries you shaped
- Generation you influenced
- Increasingly rare

**Generational Impact (50-100+ years):**
- Enduring companies
- Societal transformation
- Values that persist
- Very rare (Carnegie, Rockefeller, Jobs)

**Your Aspiration:**

## Legacy Pitfalls to Avoid

### Pitfall 1: Waiting Too Long

**Mistake**: "I'll give back after I'm successful."
**Problem**: Never feel successful enough, time runs out
**Solution**: Start now, at your current level

### Pitfall 2: Ego-Driven Legacy

**Mistake**: Buildings with your name, vanity projects
**Problem**: About you, not impact
**Solution**: Focus on solving problems, not recognition

### Pitfall 3: Neglecting Family

**Mistake**: Build empire, lose family
**Problem**: Regret when it's too late
**Solution**: Family is primary legacy, business is secondary

### Pitfall 4: Only Financial Legacy

**Mistake**: Just leave money to kids
**Problem**: Money without values creates problems
**Solution**: Pass on principles, values, work ethic

### Pitfall 5: No Succession Plan

**Mistake**: Company depends entirely on you
**Problem**: Dies or struggles when you leave
**Solution**: Build to outlive you, leadership pipeline

### Pitfall 6: Hoarding Knowledge

**Mistake**: Keep everything to yourself
**Problem**: Knowledge dies with you
**Solution**: Teach, write, mentor, share generously

### Pitfall 7: Ignoring Impact While Building

**Mistake**: "I'll worry about impact later"
**Problem**: Build in ways that create negative legacy
**Solution**: Build responsibly from day one
