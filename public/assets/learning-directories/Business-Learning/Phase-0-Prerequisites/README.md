# Phase 0: Prerequisites - Building Your Business Foundation

## Overview

Before diving into your startup journey with CoodLoom, you need to establish fundamental business knowledge and mindset. This phase prepares you for entrepreneurship with comprehensive learning materials, practical exercises, and actionable templates.

**Phase 0 is your foundation**. Everything you build in future phases relies on the knowledge, mindset, and self-awareness you develop here.

### Skills

- Strategic thinking frameworks
- Goal setting and execution planning

- Business model design and validation

### 2. [Business Fundamentals](./02-Business-Fundamentals.md)
**Time**: 6-8 hours | **Exercises**: 5 | **Priority**: üî• Critical

Master the essential business concepts you'll use daily. Covers:
- Value proposition and business models
- Revenue streams and unit economics
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC) and lifetime value (LTV)
- Financial statements basics (P&L, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow)
- Key performance indicators (KPIs) for SaaS
- Competitive analysis frameworks
- Market sizing (TAM, SAM, SOM)

**Key Exercises**:
- Complete Business Model Canvas for CoodLoom
- Calculate break-even point and runway
- Research 5 competitors and identify advantages

**Outcome**: Solid understanding of how businesses work and core terminology

---

### 3. [Tech Industry Overview](./03-Tech-Industry-Overview.md)
**Time**: 5-7 hours | **Reading**: Deep dive | **Priority**: ‚≠ê High

Understand the software and SaaS industry where CoodLoom competes. Explores:
- Software industry landscape and market size
- SaaS business model deep dive
- POS software market and competitive analysis
- Industry trends and opportunities
- Technology stack considerations
- Funding strategies (bootstrap vs VC)
- Success stories and lessons learned

**Key Sections**:
- Detailed SaaS metrics that matter
- POS competitive landscape (Square, Toast, Clover, etc.)
- Your competitive positioning opportunities
- Building a software company: key principles

**Outcome**: Comprehensive understanding of your industry and where CoodLoom fits

---

### 4. [Self-Assessment](./04-Self-Assessment.md)
**Time**: 4-6 hours | **Exercises**: 10+ | **Priority**: üî• Critical

Honestly evaluate your readiness to be a founder. Includes:
- Personal readiness assessment (motivation, time, finances)
- Personality and entrepreneurial traits evaluation
- Skills and experience inventory (technical and business)
- Co-founder relationship assessment
- Situational assessment (CoodLoom current status)
- Overall readiness score and decision framework

**Key Assessments**:
- 160-point entrepreneurial traits evaluation
- Financial readiness calculator
- Co-founder partnership scorecard
- Strengths, weaknesses, and development plan

**Outcome**: Clear picture of where you are and what you need to develop

---

### 5. [Goal Setting](./05-Goal-Setting.md)
**Time**: 4-6 hours | **Exercises**: 15+ | **Priority**: üî• Critical

Define your vision and set specific, measurable goals for CoodLoom. Covers:
- Vision and mission statement creation
- Long-term goals (3-10 years)
- Annual SMART goals
- Quarterly OKRs (Objectives and Key Results)
- Monthly milestones and weekly planning
- Metrics and tracking systems
- Accountability structures
- Personal CEO development journey

**Key Outputs**:
- CoodLoom vision, mission, and values
- 1-year SMART goals (12 specific goals)
- Q1 OKRs with measurable key results
- Month-by-month plan for next 6 months
- Metrics dashboard setup
- Commitment contract (you and co-founder)

**Outcome**: Crystal clear direction and actionable plan for CoodLoom's first year

**Week 4: Application & Practice**
- Days 1-2: Conduct 5 customer interviews
- Days 3-4: Complete financial projections
- Days 5-7: Final review and co-founder alignment meeting

### Alternative Paths

**Fast Track** (1 week, 40 hours):
- Focus on Chapters 1, 2, 4, 5 (skip Ch 3 for now)
- Complete only critical exercises
- Set up basic goal tracking
- Come back to industry deep-dive later

**Part-Time** (4-6 weeks, 10 hours/week):
- One chapter per week
- Complete exercises thoroughly
- More time for reading and reflection
- Deeper customer research

**Self-Paced** (flexible):
- Complete chapters in any order based on needs
- Focus on areas where you're weakest
- Use resources as reference materials
- Return to chapters as needed
