## Part 4: Quarterly Goals (OKRs)

### Understanding OKRs

**OKR = Objectives and Key Results**

**Objective**: What you want to achieve (qualitative, inspirational)
**Key Results**: How you'll measure success (quantitative, specific)

**Example**:
- **Objective**: Dominate the coffee shop POS market in Seattle
- **Key Result 1**: Sign 25 Seattle coffee shops
- **Key Result 2**: Achieve 4.8+ star average review score
- **Key Result 3**: Capture 15% of Seattle coffee shop market

## Part 9: Dealing with Goal Challenges

### When You're Off Track

**It Will Happen**. Here's how to handle it:

**Step 1: Diagnose Why**
- Was the goal unrealistic?
- Did priorities change?
- Were there external factors?
- Did I not execute?
- Did I lack skills/resources?

**Step 2: Decide What to Do**
- **Adjust the goal**: If unrealistic
- **Increase effort**: If execution issue
- **Change approach**: If strategy wrong
- **Deprioritize**: If no longer relevant
- **Get help**: If skill/resource gap

**Step 3: Communicate**
- Tell co-founder immediately
- Update stakeholders
- Revise plans
- Document learnings

**Step 4: Learn**
- What assumption was wrong?
- What did I underestimate?
- What will I do differently?
- How can I prevent this?

### When Goals Conflict

**Example**: "Grow fast" vs "Stay profitable"

**Resolution Process**:
1. Identify the conflict explicitly
2. Understand the trade-offs
3. Refer back to vision/values
4. Make intentional choice
5. Accept consequences
6. Revisit periodically

**Your Values Dictate Priorities**:
- If you value sustainability â†’ Profit may win
- If you value impact â†’ Growth may win
- If you value control â†’ Bootstrapping may win

**When to Consider Changing Goals**:
- âœ“ New information invalidates assumption

**When to Keep Pushing**:
